The data provided represents the number of nursing graduates across various countries (Japan, Korea, New Zealand, United States, and Mexico) from 2018 to 2022. This dataset offers insight into the annual output of nursing professionals, which is crucial for understanding each country's efforts in replenishing and expanding its healthcare workforce. Observing trends over time, Japan consistently reports high numbers of nursing graduates, peaking at 66,328 in 2018 before experiencing minor fluctuations, ending at 65,590 in 2022. The United States also maintains a high graduation rate, reaching 220,841 by 2021, indicating a steady commitment to bolstering its nursing workforce. Meanwhile, Korea shows a gradual increase in nursing graduates, climbing from 51,107 in 2018 to 54,429 in 2021, reflecting a positive trend in workforce development. Mexico, although having smaller numbers in comparison, shows variability, with a peak in 2019 at 22,129 before slightly decreasing. New Zealand's figures remain relatively low but stable, highlighting its smaller population size and healthcare system demand. A scatter plot visualization of this data would effectively display the distribution and trends of nursing graduates across countries and years, allowing for quick comparisons and revealing potential correlations between education output and healthcare workforce needs. The scatter plot can also illustrate each country's response to the growing demand for healthcare professionals, particularly in light of increasing healthcare challenges globally.
